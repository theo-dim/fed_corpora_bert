Under current law, faster productivity growth would not affect individuals who are already retired because their benefits are indexed by the consumer price index (CPI).  However, it would raise initial benefits for future retirees through its effect on real wages over time.  In the end, productivity would have to rise about 3-1/2 percent per year, about 2 percentage points per year faster than the trustees' current intermediate assumption, to eliminate the Social Security imbalance over seventy-five years; productivity growth would have to be even more rapid to achieve balance in perpetuity.
